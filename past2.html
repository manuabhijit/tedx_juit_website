HARI RAVI KUMAR

Relegion 2.0
Hari talks about exploring various dimensions of "religion", what does it means, how should we rationally observe and experience its objectivity. He focuses on redefining the term to achieve more inclusiveness to the idea of religion. A co-author of the New Bhagavad-Gita, who has also co-written three other books and several articles, deeply imbibes the knowledge about the Indian scriptures and has been inspired by them since ever.At various points in life, he has worked as a software programmer, music teacher, copy editor, product strategist, graphic designer, translator and event manager.

DHIRAJ SINGH

From lightness to darkness
After surviving the premonition in childhood, Dhiraj Singh started appreciating the value of life. Additionally, the life of people at nearby mental hospital strengthened this realization even more.But he pursued mental hospital people with an entirely different perspective.He perceived it as an idea of creative and crazy art, and with this started the journey from lightness to darkness.

MANUSHEEL GUPTA

Notes from the field
The $100 computer researcher talks of similarity between a feather and a project where you never know if you would succeed or fail. As per his view, one must simply bother about seeking a problem to completely solve it. He quotes how artists make sculptures inside the stone. Similarly, he wants us to remove the unwanted by making decisions. Talking of decisions, he describes the state of mind and heart and senses how mind is aware of the more satisfying notion which is closer to heart.But while one is attached to a particular idea, he must think critically and make use of education and hence, by striking the right balance, formulate a decision.

RAVINDER KUMAR DHIMAN

Simla -- The mine of Asian history
With the thought of celebrating 200 years of Shimla in 2015, Dr. Ravinder Kumar Dhiman started the exploration during his college years. Later on, to realize this dream, he transformed this idea into a group called Shimla Bicentennial Group. Going back in history, almost 200 years back, the Gurkha wars proved to be the major reason for establishment of Shimla. David Ochterlony, with army, seeked help and hence, 15th May 1815 is recognized as the day of establishment of Shimla as settlement. The earliest written reference of word "Semla" was found in Alexander Gerand's text of 1817.
